# Paper Learning Compass

Paper Learning Compass helps turn a research paper into beginner-friendly Obsidian notes that map the paper, its methods, and its key concepts.

## What Problem It Solves

Reading a dense paper often leaves you with scattered highlights, unclear methods, and concepts that are hard to connect later. This skill creates a clean learning map so you can review the paper, revisit the techniques it uses, and build reusable concept notes in your vault.

## How It Works

The skill reads a paper you provide, identifies the main ideas, methods, and concepts, then drafts structured Markdown notes for an Obsidian-style knowledge base. It is designed for learning and organization, not for replacing your own judgment or careful reading.

## Obsidian Vault Structure

```text
Papers/
Notes/
Methods/
Concepts/
```

## How To Use The Skill

1. Provide a single research paper or paper text that you are allowed to share.
2. Ask Codex to use Paper Learning Compass.
3. Review the generated notes before adding them to your vault.
4. Edit, rename, or expand the notes to match your own learning style.

## Example Prompt

```text
Use Paper Learning Compass on this paper. Create an Obsidian-ready note, method notes, and concept notes. Keep the explanations beginner-friendly.
```

## Output Structure

The skill may produce notes like this:

```text
Notes/Paper Title.md
Methods/Paper Title/00_Paper Title_Methods_Overview.md
Methods/Paper Title/Specific Method.md
Concepts/Paper Title/Specific Concept.md
```

## What Not To Upload

Do not upload private or sensitive material, including:

- Private PDFs
- Personal notes
- Your full Obsidian vault
- The `.obsidian` folder

Only share files or excerpts that you have permission to use with Codex.
